MỘT VÀI ĐIỀU VỀ MONSTER BOX CÓ THỂ ĐỘC GIẢ CHƯA BIẾT

0. Về những người sáng lập

Monster Box được nhen nhóm bởi hai người, Ngô Thanh Liêm và Lê Hữu Lâm. Đương nhiên tự nhận ra sức người có hạn. Hai con chim én thì không thể làm nên mùa xuân. Liêm với Lâm rủ được thêm ba người nữa, cả năm bắt đầu lập kênh youtube Monster Box đăng tải các video khoa học do nhóm xây dựng, nhưng đến video thứ sáu thì ba con én kia bay đi mất, với nhiều lý do khác nhau. Trong suốt thời gian đó họ vẫn cộng tác với anh Liêm và anh Lâm nhiều công việc khác nhau theo hướng cộng tác viên, cho đến tận ngày hôm nay.

1. Về hình vẽ trên fanpage Monster Box

Theo như quan sát, chúng tôi nhận thấy nhiều người thích phong cách minh họa của Monster Box, và một số khác chưa hài lòng. Ở đây chúng tôi muốn phân tích thêm rằng vì sao hình vẽ minh họa của Monster Box lại theo phong cách này, và quan trọng hơn, là liệu Monster Box có thể vẽ đẹp hơn thế không.

Đầu tiên, hình vẽ minh họa cho fanpage phải đáp ứng rất nhiều tiêu chí:

- Phải bắt mắt. Điều này chúng tôi tạm hài lòng dựa trên quan sát các nhận xét, đánh giá từ độc giả.

- Tất cả họa sĩ đều phải vẽ được, thậm chí phải phát triển đến mức khi nhìn vào art của Monster Box, chúng ta sẽ nghĩ là chỉ do một họa sĩ vẽ ra. Về tiêu chí này chúng tôi thừa nhận là vẫn chưa làm được hoàn hảo, mỗi họa sĩ vẫn còn bị ảnh hưởng ít nhiều phong cách cá nhân của mình, cũng như những họa sĩ mới vẫn đang cố gắng để bắt kịp theo phong cách vẽ chung này (mặc dù nếu vẽ style riêng có thể họ không thua kém gì các họa sĩ tham gia trước).

- Tiêu chí thứ ba, cũng là tiêu chí vô cùng quan trọng, phong cách này phải đảm bảo là các họa sĩ có thể vẽ đủ nhanh. Như các độc giả đã biết, bài viết Monster Box được đăng tải đều đặn hàng tuần, với mật độ tương đối dày so với công sức bỏ ra ở mỗi bài đăng (nhờ vào việc có nhiều tác giả tham gia). Cứ mỗi một tuần, chúng tôi phải đảm bảo có đủ 4 bài viết tầm bốn nghìn chữ, 4 bản dịch tiếng Anh, và 4 artwork mới. Song song đó là nhiều những sự kiện khác cần artwork. Do đó, nếu chúng tôi đưa ra một style vẽ phức tạp hơn, mất nhiều thời gian để triển khai hơn thì không có gì đảm bảo được số lượng đăng tải hàng tuần. Thay vào đó, chúng tôi tìm ra một style có thể đáp ứng hai tiêu chí phía trên, lại còn sản xuất vừa đủ nhanh. Có rất ít lựa chọn đáp ứng đủ 3 tiêu chí này.

Thời gian đầu, để đáp ứng được cả ba tiêu chí trên, chúng tôi chọn vẽ một style art nhìn giống các video của Kurzgesagt - một channel YouTube khoa học rất nổi tiếng. Đương nhiên khi nói “vẽ giống” ở đây là cùng một style art, chứ không phải chúng tôi vẽ theo những gì họ vẽ. Và đương nhiên khi nói “cùng style art” không có nghĩa là “cùng chất lượng”. Họ là người đi trước, họ vẽ đẹp hơn nhiều, và chúng tôi còn cách một đoạn rất xa. Chuyện này làm phát sinh hai vấn đề, (1) một số độc giả sẽ không thích điều này và (2) chính bản thân Monster Box sẽ không vượt qua được cái bóng của đàn anh.

Vế (1) thì chúng tôi không quan tâm lắm, vì sản phẩm cốt lõi của một fanpage khoa học là nội dung, chứ không phải hình vẽ. Chưa kể rằng chúng tôi cũng chẳng làm gì sai trái. Nhưng vế (2) là một vấn đề thực sự, do vậy chúng tôi phải tìm ra hướng đi riêng cho mình, phải tự tạo ra một style art mà khi độc giả nhìn vào thì họ nhận ra đây là sản phẩm của Monster Box, điều mà đàn anh Kurzgesagt đã làm được.

Vậy, Monster Box có thể vẽ đẹp hơn thế không, đâu là giới hạn của phong cách này? Câu trả lời là hoàn toàn có thể vẽ đẹp hơn và cho đến nay chúng tôi vẫn chưa thấy giới hạn. Vì nhu cầu phải vẽ đủ nhanh đề cập phía trên, nên thường thì họa sĩ không có quá nhiều thời gian dành cho một tấm hình đăng fanpage. Nói cách khác, những tấm hình trên fanpage hiện nay là những gì tốt nhất có thể chúng tôi đưa đến độc giả trong giới hạn cho phép, chứ không phải là những tấm hình đẹp nhất như chúng tôi mong muốn. Bạn hãy nhìn vào artwork của ngày hôm nay, đây là tấm hình được họa sĩ trau chuốt và bỏ nhiều thời gian hơn, để thấy sự khác biệt khá rõ với những tấm hình định kì. Đương nhiên, bức vẽ này cũng chưa phải là đẹp nhất, vì nó vẫn bị giới hạn thời gian. Nhưng chúng tôi cam kết rằng sắp tới, hướng đi của chúng tôi là tiến tới việc gia tăng nguồn lực để mang tới độc giả những bài viết hay hơn và những artwork đẹp hơn (hơn cả tấm đang dùng minh họa cho bài viết này), mà vẫn đáp ứng sự đều đặn định kì. Và đó là đang nói đến tương lai rất gần thôi.

2. Ở Monster Box, bạn đi làm đúng giờ, hoặc tất cả mọi thứ là vô nghĩa

Nhiều độc giả đã biết, Monster Box chỉ làm việc sáu tiếng rưỡi một ngày, và nếu bạn làm việc ngoài sáu tiếng rưỡi đó, bạn sẽ được tính chi phí OT tùy theo giờ và cộng thêm một khoản thưởng tùy theo công việc hoàn thành được vào tháng lương kế tiếp.

Vì bạn được quyền đi về để “Netflix and chill” vào lúc 3h45, nên bạn phải đảm bảo có mặt vào lúc 8 giờ sáng. Đó là nguyên tắc được chọn ở Monster Box, nguyên tắc này là phổ quát, dành cho cả 2 founder.

Anh Liêm đặc biệt không tin tưởng vào mô hình thời gian làm việc dạng “bạn đi làm mấy giờ cũng được, xong việc là được” khi xây dựng Monster Box. Mô hình đó có thể hoạt động tốt ở đâu đó, chứ không phải ở đây. Anh cho rằng đó là sự “thoải mái giả tạo” và với đặc thù công việc ở Monster Box nếu áp dụng sẽ dễ gây mất kết nối, ngoài ra thay vì tạo sự thoải mái cho mọi người, ngược lại có thể gây ra những áp lực không mong muốn và giảm năng suất hoạt động. Monster Box muốn ngoài giờ làm, tất cả mọi người sẽ được thoải mái đầu óc, có thể học thêm để nâng cao kỹ năng, chăm sóc gia đình, làm freelance thay vì mang gánh nặng đầu óc suốt cả ngày dài - từ ngày này qua ngày khác.

3. Founder là người cuối cùng trong Monster Box được tiếp cận bài viết

Đây là một trong những điều cay cú diễn ra thường xuyên mà hai founder phải chấp nhận. Thực tế hai founder chỉ được đọc bài của Monster Box cùng lúc với độc giả, theo định kì. Không hề có sự kiểm duyệt trước và sau khi đăng tải bài viết. Đương nhiên định hướng của fanpage thì tất cả sẽ cùng thống nhất, vì việc định hướng sẽ liên quan đến nhiều sản phẩm và công việc khác của Monster Box, nhưng chuyện gì diễn ra sau khi định hướng thì Biên tập viên của chúng tôi sẽ hoàn toàn là người được quyết định.

Việc hai founder năn nỉ team content để được xem trước bài viết cho đỡ sốt ruột là chuyện thường xuyên diễn ra, và số lần bị từ chối vượt trội so với số lần họ “rủ lòng thương” mà cho ngó nghiêng chút đỉnh, gọi là ngó nghiêng vì đa số những gì team content cho hai founder được tiếp cận chỉ dừng ở mức gọi là trailer. Mà xem trailer thì thường là sốt ruột hơn cả trước khi thấy bất cứ điều gì, sau đó là một tràng chỉ trích từ hai founder đến team content, nhưng chỉ trích này chỉ để share nhau xem cho vui chứ không được xem như chỉ đạo dùng để can thiệp vào bài viết. Đây có thể là một trong những thú vui của người viết bài tại Monster Box.

Bạn biết chuyện gì buồn cười hơn không? Quy tắc này được đặt ra bởi chính hai founder. Việc này liên quan đến nội dung bài viết chủ nhật tuần trước. Ở Monster Box, các bên không được can thiệp đến sản phẩm của bên còn lại, và đặc biệt nguyên tắc này được áp dụng triệt để cho những người “có quyền đó” hơn là những người không có quyền đó. Bởi chính vì sợ bản thân là người có quyền bắt các tác giả chỉnh sửa bài viết, mà lại ngứa ngáy khi đọc những thứ trái quan điểm của mình, nên điều luật này được lập ra để bảo vệ chính các tác giả.

Việc “không can thiệp nội dung fanpage” này sinh ra những hệ quả cũng buồn cười không kém là anh Liêm phải lên fanpage kêu gọi các độc giả ủng hộ mình để Monster Box duy trì các bài viết chủ nhật hàng tuần, mặc dù anh Liêm sở hữu Monster Box. Có lẽ nhờ thế bài viết ngày hôm nay các bạn đang đọc mới có thể tồn tại.

Và còn gì đau hơn nữa, khi các bạn biết rằng những bài anh Liêm viết lại phải nộp trước cho biên tập viên để họ duyệt và chấp thuận trước khi ra mắt…

4. Founder từng bị nhắc nhở hạn chế bình luận trên fanpage

Dù sở hữu Monster Box, founder không ngoại lệ hơn các độc giả. Vì fanpage là thánh địa của riêng team content, nên founder sẽ được đối xử bình thường như các độc giả. Các founder Monster Box cũng tự xếp vị trí mình như thế, cũng đã có nhiều lần bộc lộ sự bất bình quan điểm của Monster Box ngay trên fanpage, và vì cũng là người nên đôi khi đâu đó vẫn có những lời lẽ công kích không phù hợp với định hướng chung của page, và đương nhiên, như mọi độc giả, họ cũng phải đối chất với Monster Box, bị nhắc nhở hay hoặc thậm chí có nguy cơ bị ban khỏi fanpage nếu tái phạm nhiều lần.

Như một writer của Monster đã nói “ở Monster Box, bạn được viết trong không gian hào phóng về ý tưởng hơn rất nhiều những nơi khác”. Để được như vậy, giới hạn quyền lực của những người nằm ngoài chuyên môn là điều cần thiết. Hai founder thực sự biến thành độc giả - theo nghĩa đen, thay vì “tự nghĩ mình đang đứng ở vị trí độc giả và phán xét”.

Nếu bạn đang làm công việc liên quan đến sáng tạo, hoặc xây dựng sản phẩm, mà nghe câu nói cliché phổ biến này từ người có quyền hạn hơn bạn: “Tôi đang đứng ở vị trí của người dùng, và yêu cầu bạn phải sửa...” - thì xin chia buồn với bạn vì điều đó, họ không “đứng ở vị trí ở người dùng” đâu. Họ không thể, vì đơn giản là chúng ta ai chẳng đứng ở góc độ người dùng, nhưng là góc độ của một người dùng chứ không phải “toàn bộ người dùng”.

5. Monster Box luôn sợ những comment cảm tính

Có một điều khá buồn cười là rất nhiều nguời cho rằng quan điểm của họ là chân lý, nên nếu bài viết của page phù hợp quan điểm của họ, thì là “page hay, xuất sắc, bài viết nức lòng người hâm mộ, các bạn nên theo dõi page này, theo mình đánh giá là page hay nhất Việt Nam, hình ảnh đẹp…”. Còn nếu trái với quan điểm thì sẽ là một loạt những câu bình luận có cũng như không:

“Page nên tìm hiểu kỹ trước khi viết” - Câu cliché này khá phổ biến, nó không mang tinh thần phản biện, cũng như dẫn chứng gì để phản bác bài viết. Điều đáng nói là câu này rất hay được ủng hộ, vì nghe có vẻ ngầu.

“Page sai rồi vì tôi cảm thấy thế này, tôi cảm thấy thế kia” - … vâng, bạn là nhất.

“Page này cánh tả/cánh hữu à mà viết bài thế kia” - Những người này thường thể hiện sự cực đoan bên trong bình luận, họ thích chọn phe, và trong đầu họ mọi thứ hoặc diễn ra hoặc thế này hoặc thế kia. Thực ra chúng tôi luôn nghi ngờ không biết họ có hiểu rõ những định nghĩa mà họ tuyên bố hay không.

Chúng tôi sợ những comment như thế không phải vì sợ thua họ về mặt kiến thức, hoặc không trả lời được những gì họ hỏi. Cái chúng tôi sợ là sẽ làm cho không gian bình luận bị loãng, độc giả sẽ không tập trung được vào các phản biện và tranh luận nghiêm túc hơn. Và đương nhiên, cảm xúc của những writer chúng tôi mời về được cũng bị ảnh hưởng không kém.

Ở Monster Box, truyền đạt kiến thức đến độc giả và tạo ra sân chơi bổ ích là một điều được tuân theo tuyệt đối. Để làm được điều đó, bảo vệ các writer khỏi sự công kích là điều tối quan trọng. Một bài viết về toán của một chuyên gia trong lĩnh vực toán học sẽ là điều cần thiết để độc giả tiếp thu kiến thức. Mặc dù, đáng buồn là có thể là dưới comment sẽ xuất hiện câu “Nghe ai không nghe, nghe thằng phản động…” mặc dù nội dung comment chẳng có gì liên quan đến bài viết.

6. Người của Monster Box không vô thần như nhiều người lầm tưởng

Trái với công kích của mọi người rằng “Monster Box tập hợp một lũ vô thần, chỉ biết tin theo khoa học, và quan điểm thì một chiều”. Monster Box là một tập hợp những người đa dạng về tôn giáo và quan điểm. Có người theo đạo Thiên Chúa, người theo đạo Phật, người tin vào Chủ Nghĩa Tư Bản, người tin vào Chủ Nghĩa Xã Hội. Và nếu bạn chú ý thì cũng sẽ thấy các writer cũng thường xuyên cãi nhau dưới phần bình luận trên page đấy.

Tuy nhiên, niềm tin là một chuyện, mục đích của fanpage lại là chuyện khác. Monster Box là một fanpage khoa học, vậy thì những tuyên bố fanpage đưa ra phải theo khuôn khổ khoa học và tuân thủ các quy tắc tối thiểu.

7. Những cuộc đấm nhau không hồi kết

Bài viết của Monster Box phải đảm bảo các tiêu chí:

- Tuyên bố của tác giả phải có nguồn, và nguồn thì càng sơ cấp càng tốt. Nếu fanpage nhắc về một nghiên cứu có kết quả cụ thể, thì phải có nguồn cho những nghiên cứu đó, độc giả có thể tự tra cứu, kiểm chứng được và đưa ra quan điểm của riêng mình dựa trên kết quả nghiên cứu. Tuyệt đối cấm tiệt dùng một câu mơ hồ như các trang tin lá cải “Theo một nghiên cứu của các nhà khoa học ở bang Califonia…” mà không cần dẫn đến nghiên cứu ấy.

- Quan điểm cá nhân của writer phải dựa trên những tuyên bố được trích dẫn nguồn trong bài viết. Mặc dù có thể đó là quan điểm cá nhân, không được nhiều người đồng thuận, tuy nhiên cho độc giả thấy quan điểm đó rút ra từ đâu, là một điều bắt buộc.

Dù vậy, chuyện bất đồng quan điểm dẫn đến tranh luận là chuyện không thể nào tránh khỏi. Mặt khác, với chúng tôi đó là việc cần được khuyến khích, miễn là khi tranh luận chúng ta phải đáp ứng được những văn hóa tối thiểu như không được công kích cá nhân, và hạn chế mắc phải các lỗi ngụy biện logic, vân vân.

Và các bạn biết bài viết của Monster Box gây ra những cuộc tranh luận nhiều nhất là ở đâu không ? Ở nội bộ Monster Box.

Nếu bạn nghĩ những cuộc tranh luận trên fanpage đã là dữ dội, hãy biết nó chưa là gì so với các cuộc tranh luận diễn ra giữa các tác giả, nội bộ công ty, nội bộ các chuyên gia tư vấn. Khi những cuộc tranh luận rơi vào thế bế tắc, chúng tôi sẽ xử lý bằng cách lập ra một ban trọng tài, thường là từ ba đến năm người, thậm chí là toàn bộ thành viên công ty, họ sẽ bình chọn người thắng cuộc theo hình thức phổ thông đầu phiếu. Tất nhiên chỉ là một hình thức thể thao giải trí, người thắng không phải người nắm giữ chân lý, nhưng tất nhiên người thua cũng thế.

8. Màn cá độ dài bảy năm

Không phải cuộc tranh luận nào cũng có thể giải quyết dễ dàng chỉ bằng tổ trọng tài. Đặc biệt, khi cuộc tranh luận đó đã diễn ra âm ỉ chưa phân thắng bại trên bình diện giới học thuật nói chung. Như những chủ đề liên quan đến Tư bản chủ nghĩa hay Xã hội chủ Nghĩa, thế giới tất định hay bất định, nuôi mèo hay nuôi chó…

Nhưng màn cá độ dài nhất ở Monster Box cho đến nay thuộc về anh Liêm và một bạn dịch giả - chúng ta tạm gọi là bạn Huy, đơn giản vì bạn tên Huy.

Khởi nguồn màn cá độ này xuất phát từ một cuộc tranh luận xoay quanh chủ đề cũng kinh điển không kém: PC hay console đáng để mua hơn. Trong đó anh Liêm bảo vệ hệ máy console còn những người trong group thì ngược lại. Cũng như tất cả những cuộc khẩu chiến xoay quanh chủ đề này, ưu và khuyết điểm cùng một loạt các meme chọc ngoáy được hai bên ném vào nhau liên tục nhằm hạ nhục phía còn lại, ngay cả một số người anh em anh Liêm cho là thân thiết cũng không đứng về phía anh Liêm trong cuộc chiến này. Ngập tràn trong cảm giác bị phản bội, anh Liêm yếu thế hơn vì Huy có đồng minh đông đảo.

Màn khẩu chiến chỉ kết thúc khi màn cá độ diễn ra, anh Liêm cho rằng PC sẽ thất thế và về vườn khi công nghệ VR phát triển, và trong vòng bảy năm, cứ mỗi hai nhà sẽ có một nhà sở hữu kính VR. Bảy năm là khoảng thời gian tương đối ngắn, và với bối cảnh tìm đỏ mắt mới thấy một người dùng công nghệ VR như hiện nay, đương nhiên màn cá cược được chấp nhận từ phía còn lại. Một chiếc kính VR thế hệ mới nhất thời điểm đó (nếu Huy thua), hoặc một chiếc PC Gaming xịn xò trong khả năng (nếu anh Liêm thua) đã được đem ra đặt cược dưới sự giám sát của tất cả thành viên còn lại. Ai thua sẽ mua món hàng người còn lại đánh giá cao.

Nhưng đó là chuyện của 7 năm tới, còn bây giờ, có một vài điều khác mọi người vẫn chưa biết:

9. Website sắp được ra mắt!

MONSTER BOX: UNTOLD STORIES

0. Regarding our founders

Monster Box was the brainchild of 2 men - Ngô Thanh Liêm and Lê Hữu Lâm. As they quickly realized there’s a limit to what two men can do, Liêm and Lâm persuaded 3 other people to join them, and the team of five established the Monster Box Youtube channel where the group uploaded their products that were their scientific videos. But after their 6th work, 3 out of the 5 already departed from the team, for one reason or another. Still, for all these times, even till today, they still cooperated with Liêm and Lâm in various works as collaborators.

1. About the illustrations posted on Monster Box fanpage

From our observation, we noticed that many people particularly enjoyed the style of Monster Box’s illustrations, while some didn’t. Here, we would like to clarify why Monster Box chose to adopt this art style and, more importantly, can Monster Box do a better job with the illustrations.

The first thing to know is, the illustrations used for the fanpage had to meet quite a few criteria:

- They had to be captivating. And this criterion was relatively well met, especially based on the feedback from the audience.

- The style has to be doable by all of our artists; in fact, we were striving to reach the point where every single one of our illustrations looks like they’re made by the same artists. For this criteria, we have to admit that we haven’t got there yet, as the individual artists have yet been able to conceal the influence of their individuality, and the newly hired artists are still struggling to learn this uniform art style (despite how they are definitely on par with the senior artists when practicing their own styles).

- The third, and arguably the most important criterion, is that the artwork of this art style has to be quickly drawable. As you perhaps may have realized, the articles of Monster Box are uploaded at regular intervals which, in fact, are definitely quite short for the amount of work being dedicated to them (which was only doable thanks to the collaboration of many creators). Each week, we have to come up with 4 4000-word articles, the translations of those articles as well as 4 freshly-minted illustration artworks. And our artists shouldered all that while also taking on the artworks for other projects we had at the time. Thus, even if we tried to incorporate a more sophisticated art style that’s trickier to practice, there’s nothing to guarantee that it would help improve the quality of our illustrations. So instead of doing that, we came up with an art style that could fit both of the previously mentioned requirements, while also being quickly producible. And there are not so many options that fit all the 3 criteria.

In the early days, we chose to run an art style quite identical to that in the videos of Kurzgesagt - a very popular science YouTube channel. Of course, being “quite identical” meant we were using the same style of art, not that we were copying the works that they had already drawn. And when two artworks are drawn using “the same art style”, it also doesn’t necessarily mean that they are drawn with “the same quality”. Kurzgesagt are the pioneers, and they are a lot better than us; we are still quite a long way from catching up with them. This led to two problems: (1) some of our audiences might not like this state of affairs too much and (2) Monster Box might never be able to grow out of the trailblazer’s shadow if we let this continue.

As for problem (1), it’s not exactly a problem for us, because at the end of the day the core value of a fanpage is the scientific contents that it provides, and not the illustrations. And, if taken at face value, what we are doing is not wrong either, legally or morally. Problem (2), however, is definitely a problem, and a serious one at that; so we definitely felt the need to break away from Kurzegesagt’s style; we have to create for ourselves an art style that the audience, even just with the first glance, could recognize that it’s the Monster Box’s style - the precise thing that our inspirer Kurzgesagt had managed to do.

So, can Monster Box’s artworks become better, and what is the limit for this art style? The answer is definitely yes; and that even till today we still have yet to encounter its limit. Because of the necessity to produce these artworks quickly, as we mentioned, our artists would not spend too much time on any of the illustrations used for the fanpage. Meaning that the illustrations used for our fanpage these days, while indeed are the best we could provide given our current constraints and limitations, are definitely not the best this art style can provide, nor are they the best works that we hope to provide. If you take a look at the illustration for today’s article, for which our artist was allowed more time to work on, you’ll be able to perceive the difference compared to our usual artworks. Of course, even this artwork is still not the limit for us yet; because while being slightly less urgent, it was still made under a time constraint. But what we can promise is that, in the future, our plan would be to build up our capacity and resources so that we could create for our audience even better articles and artworks, in fact even better than the one we used here in this article, while still maintaining our post schedule. And by “in the future”, we mean in the near, no, very near future.

2. At Monster Box, either you arrive at work on time, or you don’t do jack

A lot of you might have known that, at Monster Box, we only work 6.5 hours per day, and if you work more than that, it will count and be paid for as hourly overtime, and the bonus for the extra deliverables you created shall also be counted for bonus payments, which will be incorporated in your upcoming monthly salary.

And in exchange for that luxury of leaving work at 3:45 p.m every day for “Netflix and chill”, you have to show up in the office at 8:00 a.m precisely. That’s the way Monster Box picked for itself, which is universally and fairly adopted, even for the 2 founders.

Mr. Liêm was particularly skeptical of the laissez-faire models like “I don’t care what time you show up and leave, as long as you finish your job” throughout the building of Monster Box. You may find that model somewhere else, but not here. Mr. Liêm believed that it was but an “illusion of comfort”; and in combination with the particularities of the works we do at Monster Box, that model would only bring disconnection within the team; and it would not only fail in creating necessary comfort for everyone but also create unwanted pressure and loss of productivity. Monster Box wants every off hours of its staff to be truly free, so that they could use those hours to learn new skills or improve the skills that they already had, to take care of their family or to take up other freelance work instead of just dragging along their unfinished work through the days, the weeks, the months.

3. The founders are the last people in Monster Box to get their hands on an article

This is a reality that our founders, while being very salty about it, have to live with. The reality is that the 2 founders can only read the articles of Monster Box at the same time that our readers do, at our usual post hour. They are not allowed any say in the creation of an article, be it before or after the article is posted. Of course, as an entity, we still have general directions, and the various operations and products of Monster Box revolve around these directions; however, what happens beyond the general directions, aka the particularities, will lie entirely in the hands of us editors.

So for the founders to get antsy and beg the content creators for a sneak peek of the article is quite the bread and butter for the company, and the times when the content team plays the fairy mothers and grants their wish could barely be counted on one hand. And even in one of those times, what we’d ever show to them barely even counts as a trailer. And, as you might have already known, the only help a trailer does is to get the fans antsier; and thus follows the founders throwing a tantrum at the content creators, but of course we only see it more as jest than serious direction for the creation. For us writers of Monster Box, this is definitely one of our rare sources of joy and happiness.

Do you know what else’s funny? This rule was created by the 2 founders themselves. Part of the reason was the issue we mentioned in the previous Sunday article. In Monster Box, the different departments are not allowed to intervene with the creation of other departments, and this rule has to be upheld even more strictly by those who are supposed to have the power to intervene than those who have not. As the founders fear themselves, as the people in positions of power, might put pressure on the writers to change their product, should they preview that piece of writing and find something contradicting their own ideas, this rule was created to protect the writers’ freedom of creativity.

This rule of “Non-intervention in the fan page's contents” also created some other much hilarious consequences, which you might have already got a glimpse of; Mr. Liêm, despite being the founder, had to show up on the fan page to ask for the audience’s opinion for himself to keep the weekly Sunday with Monster Box. If not for that, this weekly article would have been axed by the company’s procedure, and the article you’re reading would not exist.

And the best part (for us, not for him) is, Mr. Liêm’s articles have to be submitted to the editors for approval before they can be posted …

(editor: Hey! Mr. Liêm-the-writer! It's a pleasure working with you).

4. The Founders were once yellow-carded for their comments on the fan page

Despite being the owners of Monster Box, our founders don’t enjoy any privilege compared to the casual audience, when they’re in the fan page. The rule is that content creators are the boss of the fan page, and so the founders shall be treated there like any other reader. The founders also uphold this rule, so for quite a few times they also express their disagreement with the Monster Box writers in the comment section like any other readers; and, as human beings like any of us, they do sometimes get impatient and make their point in the inappropriate, ad hominem manner that’s not welcomed in the fan page, and thus reaped the consequence like any our casual readers: They got confronted by the Monster Box fan page’s administrators, got reprimanded or even faced the threat of excommunication for repetitive violations.

As said by a writer of Monster Box: “In Monster Box, as writers, you are allowed with a lot more freedom of creation than anywhere else”. And to make that possible, the isolation of the figures of authority from the department of expertise is a prerequisite. The founders are made to become a casual audience - literally so, instead of getting to “play the audience” like editors in the process of creation.

If you have ever worked in a creative or R&D industry, this scenario might be familiar to you: For a non-expert senior manager to tell you: “From a consumer’s standpoint, I think you should change it like this ...” - And if that’s the case, then you got our condolences; wherever your manager’s standing, it is not, and will never be the consumer’s standpoint. That’s impossible because while every one of us can take one single consumer’s standpoint, we cannot take that of “every consumer”.

5. Monster Box fears irrational comments

An irony is that a lot of people are just so awfully sure that whatever they’ve learned is the sole truth to the universe; so the common situation is that the page’s articles match their understanding of the world, they would be so generous with the compliments: “Amazing page!”, “Great articles!”, “I’m so glad I am a fan!”, “Hey, guys, come and check out this page!”, “The best page on Earth, for sure! Great articles, great illustrations, …!”, etc. But when our ideas don’t agree with theirs, we’ll be on the receiving end of their rage, accompanied by the utterly unconstructive comments like these:

“You guys should’ve learned better” - This one is probably the most common; it doesn’t give any point or any reasoning on why the ideas of the articles might be wrong. But nonetheless, it receives tons of upvotes anyway, probably because it makes you feel good thinking you’re better than someone else.

“You guys are wrong because I think this, and this, and this, and this, ...” - … Yeah, of course, whatever you know must be the truth to the world after all.

“How can you write something like this? Are you guys leftists/ rightists?” - These people require no effort at all to look radical; they see partisanship in everything, and for them, the world seems to have only so many sides to choose from. In fact, it’s always been a puzzle for us, whether these guys even correctly understand the names they are calling people with.

The problem with these comments is not that we are not knowledgeable enough for a debate, nor that we are afraid of getting into a debate. The real concern for us is that a debate with unqualified people might just lead to them saying endless red-herring nonsense that might divert your attention from the debates and discussions truly of value. And of course, we’re doing so to protect the feelings of our guest writers as well.

At Monster Box, “to introduce knowledge to the audiences” and “to create a healthy and educational playground” are the two principles to which we strictly adhere. To make that possible, it is absolutely crucial for the writers to be protected from being personally attacked. It is ideal for an article about mathematics to be written by an expert in mathematics, and that is precisely what Monster Box is trying to bring to the table. And in that process, we would love to not have our guest experts showered by criticism not for the scientific merit of what they’ve written, but for the political viewpoint that the readers assume they have. A scientific argument made by an expert is only wrong when it’s scientifically refuted, not when he’s labeled an “anarchist”, a “traitor to the nation”, a “postmodernist” or whatever other names you have to call him. The only thing you’d damage by throwing out those names is your own image and dignity.

6. We are not atheists

Unlike the common criticism we receive so often, that is, “Monster Box is full of atheists who worship science and refuse to consider the opposite view”, Monster Box is a group of diverse religious beliefs and political ideologies. Some of us are Christians, some are devout Buddhist; some believe in Capitalism, and some are die-hard devotees of Socialism. Paying careful attention, you may find out that our writers debate with each other a lot in our comment sections.

However, our own personal belief is one thing, and the purpose of our page is another. Monster Box is a science fanpage, so we intend for the content we make to fit the principles, the knowledge system and minimum requirements of science.

7. The non-stop gloves-off

There are a couple of criteria that an article of Monster Box has to meet:

- Every claim made by the writer must be backed by a source, the more primary the better. It means that, if the fanpage mentioned a study with a specific result, we will, without fail, cite the source to that study so our readers can read it and verify the claims that we’ve made in the article, while also formulating their own thoughts on those results. We will not say some tabloid-ish narratives like “A study conducted by scientists from California…” if we don’t have that study readily in our hands.

- Any personal conclusion made by the writer must strictly base on the claims cited from the said qualified sources. And these personal deductions, whether being well- or poorly-received by the readers, must take root in legit scientific materials, and such materials must be made known to the readers.

But even when all those principles are upheld, it’s quite clear that clashing opinions and debates are simply unavoidable as long as we are humans. And on the other hand, there are definitely merits in encouraging discussions, as long as all the parties uphold the minimum criteria to make it wholesome, namely to not commit ad hominem and other logical fallacies, etc..

And can you guess where Monster Box generates the most arguments? It is actually within our own ranks.

If you think that the debates on the fanpage is already gruesome enough, then, think again, because they are not even remotely as explosive as those between the writers, the company’s staff and our expert advisors. When one of such debates hits a dead end, we will resolve it by forming an arbitrator council, which will commonly consist of 3-5 people or, not-so-commonly, of the entire staff. The winner would then be decided by common votes. Of course, it’s more of a sport for the brain than anything, and it doesn’t necessarily ensure that the winner’s idea is the correct answer; but then the same is also true for the loser’s.

8. A seven-year-long bet

Not every debate could be resolved just by having an arbitrary council. Especially when that debate is also a smoldering, unresolved controversy even for the academic world as a whole, for example, like Capitalism vs Socialism, whether the world is determined or undetermined, whether cats or dogs make better pets, etc.

But the longest unresolved debate we’ve ever seen in Monster Box, which was then turned into a bet, was that between Mr. Liêm and one of our translators - Let’s call him Mr. Huy, because he is indeed Mr. Huy.

The bet started with a debate on an equally classic topic: What is more penny-worthy, a PC or console. As expected, Mr. Liêm was the only one to stand for the console, while the rest of the team chose otherwise. And like any other debate to ever break out on this topic, it was started by a bunch of memes on the other module’s weaknesses in order to tarnish the opponent, but this time Mr. Liêm didn’t have many allies for himself, even among his closest comrades. Betrayed and outnumbered, Mr. Liêm was completely surrounded by Mr. Huy and his allies.

The back-and-forth defamation barrage only came to an end when a bet was proposed: Mr. Liêm was convinced that PC gaming would be overrun and retired once VR technology became more developed, which would be in 7 seven-year times, and each household would own a VR set. Seven years is definitely a very ambitious deadline for such a vision, and given the reality of how rarely encountered VR technology is at present, of course, his opponent was perfectly comfortable to take on the bet. And the prize for the bet would be either the most cutting edge VR set at the time of settlement for Mr. Liêm (if he wins), or the best PC Gaming set there is for Mr. Huy (if Mr. Liêm loses), of course, both within the party’s reasonable affordability; and their winning or losing shall be arbitrated by the rest of the Monster Box team. Winner winner chicken dinner!

But then we’ll have to wait 7 more years to find out how that’ll settle. Now, to a couple of other untolds:
